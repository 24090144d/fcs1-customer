# CO / CCO Dashboard Reference

Source of truth: `components/dashboard/CoDashboardView.tsx` (`buildKpis`, `buildCharts` for hotel `co-01..42`, `buildCorpCharts` for corp `cco-01..46`, `benchmarkLinesForKpi`). Titles/notes below are the live i18n text (`chart_titles_co`/`chart_notes_co`, English). CO has by far the largest chart set of any module (88 charts total), so this reference uses compact tables rather than per-chart sections — every requested field (Name, Chart Type, Notes, Formula, Good/Bad) is still present as a column.

**CO and CCO share the exact same 10 KPI definitions** (`buildKpis` is one function used for both hotel and corp scope — the only difference is which rows are passed in).

**`${clause}`** in a formula = the currently active filter clause (hotel/floor/attendant/room-type/status/date-range); **`${suffix}`** in a note = a human-readable summary of that same active filter. Both are runtime-filled placeholders, not literal text shown to the user.

**Long Charts membership** (`LONG_CHART_IDS`): `co-06, co-22, co-24, co-26, co-27, co-30, co-33, co-36, co-39, co-40, co-41, co-42, cco-25, cco-27, cco-29, cco-30, cco-33, cco-36, cco-39, cco-42, cco-43, cco-44, cco-45, cco-46`. Everything else renders in Simple Charts.

**Known title/content mismatch found while compiling this doc:** `co-08`'s live title (`chart_titles_co.co-08`) reads "Top 10 Attendants by Completed Orders," but the chart's actual code-level title/note/formula (`'Top 10 Attendants by Completed Credit vs Orders'`, dual-axis line: order count vs cleaning credit) is about credit *and* orders together, not just an order-count ranking. Same class of stale-i18n-title issue documented for JO's jo-16/17/21.

## 0. Recommended: Highest Business Value (4 KPIs + 4 Charts)

### KPIs

| Rank | KPI | Why it's top-tier |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **co_completion_rate — Completion Rate** | The outcome metric every other CO KPI explains the "why" behind — did the room actually get cleaned. |
| 2 | **co_on_time_rate — On-Time Completion Rate** | Directly gates check-in: a completed-but-late clean still leaves a guest waiting at the front desk. This is the metric with the most immediate guest-facing consequence of anything in the CO set. |
| 3 | **co_reclean_rate — Re-clean Rate** | A quality failure metric, not a speed one — captures work that had to be redone, which duration/completion metrics can't see. High re-clean quietly inflates every duration number downstream. |
| 4 | **co_productivity_score — Attendant Productivity Score** | The only KPI that ties output (credit-weighted) to labor cost (hours) — the number finance and ops actually use to judge staffing efficiency, not just service quality. |

Deliberately excluded: co_total_orders/co_completed_orders are scale-dependent volume counters; co_avg_duration/co_median_duration are useful but are essentially two views of the same underlying signal that on-time rate already captures at the SLA boundary; co_delayed_orders is the count-form of on-time rate (redundant once you have the rate); co_inspection_pass_rate is close to re-clean rate in what it measures (quality) and the two often move together.

### Charts

| Rank | Chart | Why it's top-tier |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **cco-15 — Hotel Readiness Risk Index** | The only chart in the entire CO/CCO set that's a *composite* score (completion gap + delayed + behind + re-clean, weighted) — built specifically to answer "which hotel needs help right now" in one glance, rather than making an ops director cross-reference five separate charts. |
| 2 | **co-39/cco-43 — Floor → Room Attendant → Average Cleaning Duration** | The most actionable individual-accountability chart: goes from a physical location (floor) to the specific attendant to their actual average duration, split by room type. Turns "housekeeping is slow" into "this attendant on this floor is slow on suites specifically." |
| 3 | **co-10/cco-09 — Re-clean / Inspection Result Analysis** | Combines two quality signals (re-clean pressure and inspection fail rate) in one view — catches rework and wasted labor that a pure completion/duration chart would miss entirely. |
| 4 | **cco-08 — On-Time vs Delayed by Hotel** | The fastest chain-wide executive scan for the KPI that matters most operationally (on-time rate) — one bar per hotel against a clear ≥90%/<80% threshold, immediately flagging which property is causing check-in delays today. |

Honorable mention: **co-14/cco-16 (heatmap of credit/orders by hour × floor/hotel)** is a genuinely useful staffing-imbalance finder but didn't make the top 4 because co-39/cco-43 already answers the "where is the problem" question more directly, down to the individual.

## 1. KPIs (10 — shared by CO and CCO)

| ID | Name | Notes | Formula | Good | Watch | Bad |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| co_total_orders | Total Cleaning Orders | Total cleaning order records in the selected period. | `COUNT(*) WHERE {filters}` | — | — | Scale-dependent; compare with same hotel history. |
| co_completed_orders | Completed Orders | Orders that reached completion during the selected period. | `COUNT(*) FILTER status_normalized = 'Completed'` | — | — | Scale-dependent; compare with same hotel history. |
| co_completion_rate | Completion Rate | Share of cleaning orders completed in the selected period. | `Completed Orders / Total Orders * 100` | ≥ 95% | 90–94.9% | < 90% |
| co_avg_duration | Average Cleaning Duration | Average completion time for completed cleaning orders. | `AVG(actual_duration_minutes)` | ≤ 40 min | 40–44 min | > 44 min |
| co_median_duration | Median Cleaning Duration | Median completion time, which reduces distortion from outliers. | `MEDIAN(actual_duration_minutes)` | ≤ 40 min | 40–44 min | > 44 min |
| co_on_time_rate | On-Time Completion Rate | Share of completed cleaning orders finished on or before the planned time. | `On-Time Completed / Completed * 100` | ≥ 90% | 80–89.9% | < 80% |
| co_delayed_orders | Delayed Orders | Completed orders that missed the planned completion window. | `COUNT(*) FILTER status = 'Delayed'` | ≤ 5% (rate) | 5–10% | > 10% |
| co_reclean_rate | Re-clean Rate | Share of cleaning orders that triggered a re-clean requirement. | `Re-clean Orders / Total Orders * 100` | ≤ 5% | 5–10% | > 10% |
| co_inspection_pass_rate | Inspection Pass Rate | Share of inspected orders that passed inspection. | `Inspection Pass / Inspected * 100` | ≥ 95% | 90–94.9% | < 90% |
| co_productivity_score | Attendant Productivity Score | Credit-weighted output per labor hour, normalized to a score for comparison. | `SUM(cleaning_credit) / SUM(labor_hours) * 100` | Improving vs prior period | Flat vs prior period | Declining vs prior period |

Note: `co_delayed_orders` displays as a raw count on the KPI card, but its status/tone is computed from the equivalent *rate* (delayed/total) against the 5%/10% thresholds shown above — the card and its underlying classification use different units.
